Butter Chicken (Instant Pot)

Serves 8 · 110 min total

indianinstant-potkid-friendlyleftover-friendly

Ingredients

  • 3 lb chicken breast — boneless skinless, cut into ½-inch pieces
  • 1 cup Greek yogurt — marinade
  • 2 Tbsp lemon juice — marinade
  • 8 tsp garam masala — divided (marinade + sauce)
  • 2 tsp smoked paprika — divided (marinade + sauce)
  • 1½ tsp turmeric — divided (marinade + sauce)
  • 4 tsp fresh ginger — grated, divided
  • 8 cloves garlic — divided (marinade + sauce)
  • 2½ tsp salt — divided
  • 4 Tbsp cooking oil
  • 6 Tbsp butter
  • 2 yellow onion — finely diced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin — sauce
  • 2 can tomato sauce — 15 oz each
  • ½ cup water
  • ⅔ cup heavy cream — room temperature

Steps

  1. Cut the chicken into small ½-inch pieces. In a large bowl, whisk the marinade: Greek yogurt, lemon juice, 2 tsp grated ginger, 4 cloves pressed garlic, 4 tsp garam masala, 1 tsp smoked paprika, 1 tsp turmeric, and 1½ tsp salt.
  2. Add the chicken to the marinade and coat evenly. Marinate in the refrigerator at least 1 hour (or overnight). Take it out 10 minutes before cooking.
  3. Set the Instant Pot to Sauté (high). Add the cooking oil and sear the marinated chicken in batches, ~4–5 minutes, just to brown — it won't be fully cooked yet. Remove to a plate.
  4. Still on Sauté, melt the butter, then cook the diced onion about 4 minutes until soft. Add 4 cloves minced garlic, 2 tsp grated ginger, and the rest of the spices (4 tsp garam masala, cumin, 1 tsp smoked paprika, 1 tsp salt, ½ tsp turmeric). Stir and cook 1 minute until fragrant.
  5. Pour in the water and scrape the bottom of the pot well to lift any browned bits (this prevents a burn warning). Stir in the tomato sauce, then return the chicken and any juices.
  6. Cancel Sauté. Lock the lid, set the valve to Sealing, and cook on High Pressure for 8 minutes. Let the pressure release naturally for 10 minutes, then quick-release the rest.
  7. Switch to Keep Warm / low Sauté and stir in the room-temperature heavy cream until heated through, 2–3 minutes (don't let it boil). Garnish with cilantro and serve over basmati rice or with naan.
